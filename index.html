Bruce Lee

A legendary kung-fu master

Major events in Bruce Lee's life

  • 1940 - Born in San Francisco, California, U.S.A
  • 1941 - Relocated to Hong Kong with his family
  • 1950 - Co-starred with his father in "The Kid"
  • 1953 - Began training in Wing Chun with Yip Man, a Wing Chun master, in HongKong
  • 1958 - Won the Hong Kong schools boxing tournament
  • 1958 - Enrolled at the University of Washington and studied dramatic arts, philosophy and psychology etc.
  • 1959 - Began giving lessons in martial arts to students in his neighborhood; established a martial arts school called the Lee Jun Fan Gung Fu Institute
  • 1964 - Met Linda Emery and got married with her
  • 1964 - Took part in the Long Beach International Karate Championships, where he won the audience with his two-finger push-ups.
  • 1966 - Started his first major Hollywood movie, casting as Kato in the hit series titled The Green Hornet
  • 1971 - Signed by Shaw Brothers Studio and Golden Harvest as a lead in two films – ‘The Big Boss’ and ‘Fists of Fury’ (1972)
  • 1972 - Partnered with Chow to start up a production company called Concord Production Inc
  • 1972 - Accepted to star in Warner Brothers’ Enter the Dragon
  • 1973 - Filmed Enter the Dragon begins in Hong Kong
  • 1973 July -- Died of an allergic reaction to pain killers and was laid to rest in Lakeview Cemetery, Seattle.
